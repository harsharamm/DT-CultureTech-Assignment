# Question 1 — Sourcing Methods

## 1. DSIR Recognized R&D Units
The DSIR database contains companies with recognized in-house R&D facilities. This is useful because companies investing in R&D usually match the Federer profile of technical and differentiated manufacturers.

Why it works:
- Signals technical depth
- Strong indicator for differentiated manufacturing
- Good source for biotech, chemicals, defence, and electronics

Limitations:
- Misses newer startups
- Some companies may no longer be active

---

## 2. Industry Expo Exhibitor Lists
Trade expos such as:
- BioAsia
- CPHI India
- Aero India
- IMTEX
- ELECRAMA

Why it works:
- Companies paying for exhibition booths are usually growth-oriented
- Easy way to identify active manufacturers
- Helps discover niche specialty manufacturers

Limitations:
- Smaller MSMEs may not participate
- Some exhibitors are distributors instead of manufacturers

---

## 3. BSE SME and NSE Emerge
These platforms contain listed SME manufacturing companies.

Why it works:
- Public financial data available
- Leadership information is easier to verify
- Revenue bands are visible

Limitations:
- Only covers listed companies
- Many MSMEs remain private

---

## 4. MCA Filings
Using MCA company data and NIC codes to identify manufacturing companies.

Why it works:
- Large searchable database
- Useful for identifying manufacturing clusters
- Helps discover hidden MSMEs

Limitations:
- Data can be noisy
- Some companies may be inactive

---

## 5. LinkedIn Sales Navigator
Searching founders and technical leaders directly.

Why it works:
- Helps identify technical decision makers
- Good for finding promoter-driven businesses
- Useful for hiring and growth signals

Limitations:
- Self-reported information
- Not all MSMEs maintain active LinkedIn profiles

Build a verified database of 1000 ICP-qualified companies within one month.

---

# Step 1 — Build Initial Company Universe
Collect around 3500–4000 companies using:
- DSIR lists
- Expo directories
- BSE SME
- MCA data
- LinkedIn
- Industry associations

Expected output:
- Company names
- Websites
- City
- Segment

---

# Step 2 — Initial Filtering
Auto-remove:
- Traders
- Import/export companies
- CRO/service companies
- PE-owned businesses
- Companies above Rs.500Cr revenue
- Inactive companies

Expected remaining:
2000–2500 companies

---

# Step 3 — AI Assisted Qualification
Use AI tools to:
- Analyze websites
- Detect manufacturing evidence
- Identify technical founders
- Find certifications and growth signals
- Score companies using Federer criteria

Expected output:
1200–1400 probable companies

---

# Step 4 — Manual Verification
Human review for:
- Borderline companies
- Ownership verification
- Manufacturing verification
- Technical founder validation
- Revenue cross-checking

Expected final list:
1000 verified companies

---

# Quality Control

To reduce hallucinations:
- Cross-check AI output with official company websites
- Verify revenue through BSE/MCA
- Confirm certifications manually
- Reject companies with weak evidence
- Re-score borderline companies manually
